Blog | BIM tools: For synergy in design coordination

December 2023

Design coordination among various trades, such as architecture, structure, mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP), is a critical aspect of the construction process. Traditionally, coordinating these diverse elements involved cumbersome manual efforts, leading to inefficiencies, errors, and delays. However, the advent of Building Information Modeling (BIM) technology has transformed the way professionals approach design coordination.

Design Coordination:
Design coordination involves ensuring seamless collaboration among different disciplines to avoid clashes and conflicts in the construction phase. It requires aligning architectural, structural, and MEP designs to create a harmonious and efficient construction plan.

Tools for Design Coordination:
BIM technology, coupled with advanced software like Navisworks and Solibri, has revolutionized the construction industry. These tools enable real-time collaboration and precise clash detection during the design coordination process. Navisworks and Solibri facilitate the integration of multidisciplinary models, allowing teams to identify clashes, interferences, and inconsistencies early in the design phase.

Major Benefits:
The adoption of BIM in design coordination offers significant advantages over conventional practices. One of the primary benefits is the substantial savings in terms of cost and time. BIM allows for better visualization, enhanced communication, and streamlined workflows, resulting in faster decision-making and reduced rework.

Clash Detection:
Clash detection is a pivotal component of BIM, enabling the identification and resolution of clashes between different building components. This process minimizes on-site clashes during construction, reducing the likelihood of costly errors and modifications. By addressing clashes in the virtual environment, BIM technology ensures a smoother construction process and enhances overall project efficiency.

In conclusion, the integration of BIM technology and software applications like Navisworks and Solibri has ushered in a new era of precision and efficiency in design coordination. The ability to detect clashes in the virtual realm not only saves costs and time but also contributes to the overall success of construction projects. As the construction industry continues to evolve, BIM remains at the forefront, reshaping the way professionals collaborate and coordinate designs across various trades. Embracing BIM technology is no longer an option but a necessity for those looking to stay competitive and deliver projects with unparalleled accuracy and efficiency.
